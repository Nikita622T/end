About Us

History of
Carpathians

The Carpathians are a mountain system in Eastern Europe on the territory of the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Ukraine, Hungary, Poland, Romania, Serbia and partly Austria (Hundsheimer Berge near Hainburg an der Donau and Niederosterreichisch Inselbergschwelle near Mistelbach).According to Roman chronicles of the 1st century AD. e., in this area lived the Dacian tribe of carp. Historians and linguists find it difficult to answer the question: the tribe is named for the mountains, or vice versa.In the era of Antiquity and the Middle Ages, the Carpathians were called the Sarmatian mountains. Also called Ugric (Hungarian).
